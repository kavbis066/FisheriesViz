Data Story

The line graph shows the amount of fish that has been disposed in the form of canning over the years from around 1991 to 2017.

What is canning?
Canning is a way of preserving food items by sealing them in air tight containers, in this context fishes are the ones which are tightly packed and sealed in cans or containers.

The amount of canned fishes have shown varies trends over the years. In 1991 30.10 tonnes of fishes were canned and in 2017 the amount of fishes that were canned was 36.15 tonnes. Over the years as we can see from the graph there were dips in the amount canned in the years 1993, 2002, 2005, 2011 and 2017. On the hand there was tremendous increase in the years 2000,2003,2004,2010 and 2015.

Even though Andaman and Nicobar Island has the longest coastline of India there was comparatively a smaller number of fisher folk population. Tamil Nadu having the highest number of fisher folk population. The irony is that Andhra Pradesh is at the top of fish production while it has relatively lower number of fisher folk population than Tamil Nadu.

Dataset

“Handbook on Fisheries Statistics - 2018” provided by the Union Ministry of Fisheries helped us in knowing different areas which are the focus of the department of fisheries in India. There are a range of sections this handbook covers from production of fish to fisheries institutes and other factors like exports, fishing harbours and much more. In its true sense this handbook gives proper details for every type of individuals, such as a student wants to pursue Fisheries science then which institute to choose and type of courses are also mentioned, for people want to know about the export of fisheries and expenditure on fishery then they could find it in this handbook.
